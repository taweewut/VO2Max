What is VO2max?

This section breaks down the core concept of VO2max. It's the "gold standard" measure of your cardiorespiratory fitness, indicating how effectively your body uses oxygen during intense exercise.

A Fundamental Metric

VO2max, or maximal oxygen uptake, is the maximum rate (V) of oxygen (O2) your body can consume during peak exertion. It's a powerful indicator of your aerobic "engine size."

An Integrated System

VO2max depends on the coordinated effort of your lungs, heart, blood, and muscles.

Why It Matters

Discover why improving your VO2max is crucial. It's not just for elite athletes; a higher VO2max is one of the strongest predictors of long-term health and longevity, while also being the foundation for endurance performance.

Health & Longevity

VO2max naturally declines with age. However, regular exercise can significantly slow this decline. A higher VO2max is strongly linked to a lower risk of chronic diseases and increased lifespan.

How to Improve It

Your VO2max is highly trainable. This section provides evidence-based strategies, from specific workout structures to lifestyle factors, that will help you boost your aerobic capacity, with potential gains of 15-20%.

Training Strategies

A mix of high-intensity work and a strong aerobic base is the key. The "80/20 rule" is a popular model: 80% easy, 20% hard.

HIIT is the most effective way to boost VO2max. These workouts involve short, hard efforts near your maximum capacity, followed by recovery.

  • Norwegian 4x4: 4 rounds of 4 mins at 90-95% max HR, with 3 mins active recovery.
  • Short Repeats: 6-10 x 400m at your 1500m-3K race pace.
  • Hill Repeats: 5 x 3 minutes running hard uphill.

This is the foundation of your fitness. Long, steady-state runs at a conversational pace (60-70% max HR) build endurance and support your ability to handle harder workouts.
